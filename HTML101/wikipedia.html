Computer Science

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Computer Science is the study of computers, computational systems, software, and algorithms. It focuses on how information is processed, stored, and communicated using technology.

Introduction

Computer Science is an academic discipline that studies computation, programming, data processing, and computer systems. It is one of the fastest-growing fields in the world.

History

The roots of Computer Science can be traced to mathematics and logic. Charles Babbage designed the Analytical Engine in the 19th century, while Alan Turing laid the foundations of modern computing in the 20th century.

Main Areas

  • Programming
  • Software Engineering
  • Database Systems
  • Computer Networks
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Cybersecurity

Applications

Computer Science is used in healthcare, education, banking, communication, entertainment, transportation, and scientific research. Modern technologies such as smartphones, websites, and artificial intelligence depend on computer science principles.
